Across the globe, numerous resources exist to shine a light on money, politics, access and influence. Even with limited data on purely political money—campaign contributions and expenditures—creative ways exist to better understand the influence industry. We urge you to let us know of any additional sources of information to include here.


Global Tools to Explore Money, Politics and Influence


  • Ad Hawk
    USA

    This mobile app is a product of the Sunlight Foundation that aggregates data on political ads from a variety of sources and allows users to identify who is backing ads in an attempt to influence the vote.

  • aHÁLÓ
    Hungary

    This searchable database of individuals, institutions and subsidies is a joint-project by K-Monitor, Transparency International Hungary and partners for the Association for Human Rights that brings greater transparency in public spending and decision-making in Hungary.

  • As Claras
    Brazil

    Run by Transparencia Brasil, this website has a database on all political finance information in Brazil.

  • Cuentas Claras
    Columbia

    Web-based tool with the support of numerous groups, Cuentas Claras is used to submit campaign finance information, and contains a searchable database of political finance information.

  • Dinero y Política
    Argentina

    An initiative of Poder Ciudadano Foundation, Dinero y Politica is an online database which aggregates political finance data from 23 different provincial databases and 713 recognized political parties to help others investigate and make meaningful comparisons on influence.

  • FollowTheMoney.org
    USA

    FollowTheMoney.org, a project of the The National Institute on Money in State Politics compiles political finance information in all 50 states, and makes it publicly available and searchable.

  • Hypocrisy Campaign
    Hungary

    A joint initiative of Transparency International Hungary and K-Monitor to monitor the campaigns of the main political parties during the 2014 parliamentary election and to share the information with the wider public on estimated costs of campaigns through a real-time repository of collected data.

  • Influence Explorer
    USA

    Influence Explorer provides an overview of campaign finance, lobbying, earmark, contractor misconduct and federal spending data in the US. Influence Explorer is a project of the Sunlight Foundation.

  • Inspector de Intereses
    Chile

    This Fundacion Ciudadano Inteligente project lets citizens and journalists cross-reference data on Chilean members of parliament with potential conflicts of interest depending on their policy area and economic interests.

  • MAPLight
    USA

    MAPLight provides journalists and citizens with transparency tools that connect data on campaign contributions, politicians, legislative votes, industries, companies, and more to show patterns of influence.

  • Open Duka
    Kenya

    Open Duka, a project of the Open Institute, maps relationships from various sources, including shareholder, procurement and company data to help citizens, journalists, and civic activists understand the ownership structure of Kenyan entities.

  • Money, Government and Politics
    Albania

    The Albanian Institute of Sciences aims to provide access to visualizations and well-structured data on assets declarations, public assets and procurements, public spending data, fiscal data, electoral financing data and data on corporations financed by public funds to compare potential conflicts of interest over time.

  • Open Interests
    EU

    Open Interests explores the relationship between lobbying, expert consultations, public expenditure and procurement in the EU. The project mines and aggregates bulk data from a variety of sources about key actors and their policy agendas.

  • Open Secrets
    USA

    OpenSecrets.org is the Center for Responsive Politics’ website that tracks the influence of money on US politics, and how that money affects policy and citizens' lives.

  • PartidosTransparentes
    Chile

    A project of Transparency International Chile, PartidosTransparentes is an index that monitors and measures the transparency of Chilean political parties.

  • Poderopedia
    Chile

    This collaborative platform uses data visualization and technology to help citizens understand the relationships between people, companies and organizations that influence policy in Chile, Colombia and Venezuela.

  • Political Finance Monitoring Project
    Georgia

    Transparency International Georgia’s on-going effort to monitor the contributions, spending and activities of political parties and campaigns through data collection and advocacy.

  • Political Party Time
    USA

    Political Party Time, a product of the Sunlight Foundation, uses crowdsourced data to document the political fundraising circuit in the US.

  • PolitickeFinance.cz
    Czech Republic

    PolitickeFinance.cz, by the Center for Applied Economics, is a searchable database that visualizes donations made to parties and visualizes them in a user-friendly fashion.

  • El Precio del Poder
    Argentina

    An activist uses officially published information to conduct a complex analysis of the characteristics of political financing in Argentina.

  • Quién Manda
    Spain

    Quién Manda, a project by Civio, is a map of political and economic power in Spain, that combines data research, photographs and investigative journalism to document a network of the most influential people in the country.

  • La Ruta Del Dinero
    Argentina

    A group of journalists and hackers launched a contest to promote the use of data to track “ the route of public money,” and are using political finance data to create a searching and analysis tool for journalists. Though based in Argentina, there is also focus on Chile, Peru, Bogota, Mexico, and Miami among other countries and municipalities.

  • La Ruta Del Electoral
    Argentina

    La Ruta Electoral, a joint project from Poder Ciudadano, CIPPEC, LaNacion and Andy Tow, tracks and visualizes money donated to electoral campaigns from 2007 to 2013. It displays information on an individual donor's record, but also aggregates this data to make meaningful conclusions about donations more generally.

  • Who pays?
    Uruguay

    This political finance database, created by Sudestada.uy, scrapes public records on political funding and visualizes them in a searchable database. Sudestada.uy blends this data with journalistic storytelling to reveal how political funding is shaping the political system.

Studies and Recommendations on Political Finance Transparency


Global

  • If Money Talks, What Does it Say? Corruption and Business Financing of Political Parties
    Iain McMenamin, 2013

    Based on intensive case studies of Australia, Canada and Germany, this book places business financing of political parties in the contexts of debates about political corruption.

  • The Funding of Political Parties and Election Campaigns: A Handbook on Political Finance
    International IDEA, 2014

    Addresses the problems of money in politics by analyzing political finance regulations around the world and providing guidance for reform.

  • Money in Politics: A Study of Party Financing Practices in 22 Countries
    National Democratic Institute, 2005

    An in-depth comparison of party financing and corruption across 22 countries.

  • Monitoring Election Campaign Finance: A Handbook for NGOs
    Open Society Foundations, 2005

    A handbook with several case studies and strategies for effectively monitoring parties and candidates financing.

  • Opening Government: A Guide to Best Practice in Transparency, Accountability and Civic Engagement Across the Public Sector
    Transparency and Accountability Initiative, 2011

    A guide to best practice in transparency, accountability and civic engagement across the public sector.

  • Transparency and Integrity in Political Finance
     Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), 2012

    A analysis of trends in political finance regulations in its Member countries, making recommendations for best practices.

Europe

  • Fighting Corruption: Political Funding
    Council of Europe’s Group of States against corruption (GRECO)

    The Council of Europe’s Group of States Against Corruption (GRECO) evaluates the transparency of political party funding in Europe and draws general lessons.

  • Policy Position: Standards on Political Funding and Favours
    Transparency International, 2009

    Policy standards to ensure that elected politicians make decisions in the broader public interest and not to the benefit of those that funded their ascent to power.

  • The Funding and Oversight of Political Parties and Election Campaigns in East Central Europe
    Frank Bold, 2015

    Drawing upon three post-communist states—the Czech Republic, Slovakia, and Poland, this study presents a comparison of legislation surrounding political parties’ finances as well as quantitative and qualitative data on the practices of political campaigning within the region.

  • Political Party Integrity: More Accountable, More Democratic
    Transparency International, 2012

    A discussion of how transparency can help respond to integrity deficits in political parties.

  • Recommendations of the Committee of Ministers to Member states
    Council of Europe, 2003

    The Council of Europe puts forth recommendations to combat corruption in the funding of political parties and electoral campaigns.

Asia

  • Reforming Political Financing
    Malaysia

    An examination of the impact of political finance reform in Malaysia.

North America

  • Campaign Disclosure, Privacy and Transparency
    William and Mary Bill of Rights Journal, 2011, USA

    An examination of the tension between privacy and transparency in the US in the age of electronic reporting of campaign finance disclosure.

  • From the Public Interest to the Poisoned Subsidy? Public funding of political parties in democratizing countries
    Harvard University, 2006, Mexico

    Using Mexico’s generously publicly-funded party system, this paper begins to address how public funding can affect electoral outcomes.

South America

  • Inter-American Convention Against Corruption

    These measures, adopted in 1996 in Venezuela, put forth a framework to prevent, detect, punish and eradicate corruption in the Americas.

Africa

  • Guidelines for African Union Electoral Observations and Monitoring Missions
    African Commission on Human and People’s Rights

    These principles are intended to provide guidelines for African countries as they carry out and monitor democratic elections.


Evaluating Regulations: Political Finance Databases


  • Perceptions of Electoral Integrity
    The Electoral Integrity Project

    This study includes data on campaign finance released every year for all presidential and parliamentary elections around the world.

  • Global Integrity Report
    Global Integrity

    A periodic evaluation of transparency policies and implementation of these policies, including political finance, in countries all over the globe.

  • Key Findings from the Global Integrity Report
    Global Integrity

    A cross-country analysis of all data generated by the Global Integrity reports.

  • Political Finance Database
    International Institute for Democracy and Electoral Assistance (IDEA)

    A comprehensive database on national political finance regulations from 180 countries.

  • Political Finance Regulations Around the World
    International Institute for Democracy and Electoral Assistance (IDEA)

    An overview of the International IDEA Database, highlight key trends and correlations between political finance regulation and other variables.
